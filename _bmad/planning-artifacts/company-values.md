# What Duble Slash believes

**Status:** Draft. Tal flagged the current wording as "really bad" on 2026-04-21 and will revise. See [task-board.md](./task-board.md) → PRD-004.

Source brainstorm: [brainstorm-company-values.md](./brainstorm-company-values.md).

---

## 1. Flows, not frames.

A frame is a single moment. A flow is everything that happens before it, around it, and after it. We don't believe you've designed something until you've designed the path through it.

## 2. The best tools don't ask you to switch.

We don't think anyone should have to abandon the software they already love to work with AI. A tool that asks you to change your whole setup to use it isn't a tool, it's a tax. We meet you inside your workflow, not outside it.

## 3. Human-AI collaboration needs its own method.

Agile was designed for humans coordinating with humans. It breaks down when AI is in the room as a collaborator, not just a subject. That's not a small gap — it's why so much AI work feels chaotic right now. FISH is our answer.

## 4. Ownership comes from participation.

Work you didn't touch isn't yours — and you can feel it. You don't trust it, you can't defend it, you wouldn't sign your name to it. We don't believe in handing the work off to an agent and coming back when it's done. The human makes the calls that matter. That's what makes it theirs.

## 5. Trust is earned by showing the work.

We don't think users should have to take anyone's word for anything — ours or the AI's. Every step should be visible. Every decision should be reviewable. If we ever have to say "just trust us," something has already gone wrong.
