---

## Guiding principles (what makes a good Duble Slash sticker)

1. **Either pure brand OR insider one-liner.** No middle. "//" works. "We help teams ship faster with AI" does not.
2. **Designers want to signal taste** when they put something on their laptop. The sticker must flatter the *viewer's* discernment, not flatter Duble Slash.
3. **Typographic play beats illustration** for this audience — they notice kerning and will roll their eyes at another cartoon mascot.
4. **Use the vocabulary as if everyone already knows it.** Don't explain Salmon; let the curious Google it. Mystery is marketing.
5. **Avoid emoji-heavy or stacked-clause copy.** Two words > five words > a sentence.

---

## Top picks (curated for first print run)

If you print 6 designs, start here:

1. **//** — pure mark, 3" square, black on holographic foil. The hero. Costs nothing to design, everyone puts it on.
2. **// nudge, don't refuse** — mono/code aesthetic, dark background. Designer-philosophical; opens the conversation.
3. **Nemo. Tuna. Salmon. Willy.** — four-color horizontal strip, no explanation. Mystery = Google = landing page.
4. **Method > Model** — high-contrast serif or condensed sans. The whole pitch in two words.
5. **See you in the handoff.** — monospace. Only the initiated will get it; the initiated are your top-of-funnel.
6. **Grant your AI taste.** — the shortened version of Tal's seed. Flatters designers (implies *they* have taste to grant).

## Design-team meeting-specific notes

- **Bring 4–6 designs, not 20.** Designers want to choose; too many reads as low-taste.
- **At least one "pure brand" option** (`//`). People who don't get the joke still want the mark.
- **At least one "insider joke" option** (`// nudge, don't refuse` or `See you in the handoff.`). The ones who get it become your evangelists.
- **One collect-all set** (four phases or four fish). Creates a return hook.
- **Print quality matters for this audience.** Holographic, foil, or true matte black-on-black will land; cheap glossy white-stock will not.
- **Consider a single-slash tiny companion** (`/`) at 1" — people put tiny stickers next to the big ones; the `/` next to the `//` is a subtle "you belong here" signal.
